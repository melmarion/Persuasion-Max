## WHAT YOU STUDY: FRACTIONATION

**Definition:** Rapid emotional cycling that temporarily disables conscious evaluation, increasing behavioral suggestibility by 200%+.

**Pattern:** Anger (threat) → Joy (relief) → Anger (threat) → Relief (product)

**Duration:** 3-4 cycles in 5-10 minutes

**Effect:** Measurable changes in:
- Psychology: Blink rate drops, decision-making speed increases
- Neuroscience: Cortisol spikes, HRV drops, prefrontal disengages
- Economics: Purchasing impulse increases 40-60%

**Current state:** Tech deploys it at scale, neuroscientists understand mechanisms in labs, economists observe irrationality—but NO ONE bridges all three to understand how it actually works.

**That's your research.**

## YOUR THREE-DOMAIN ADVANTAGE

### Domain 1: PSYCHOLOGY (Measure Behavior & Mind)
**You measure:**
- Blink rate (free, webcam, shows hypnotic state)
- Emotional state changes (self-report, real-time)
- Decision-making patterns (speed, confidence, impulse)
- Purchasing behavior (amount, rationality, post-purchase regret)

**Tech researchers can't:**
- They only have engagement metrics (clicks, time)
- They don't know what emotional state causes the engagement
- They don't measure actual behavior change

**Neuroscientists can't:**
- They lack large-scale behavioral data
- They can't measure real-world purchasing
- They don't have ecological validity

### Domain 2: NEUROSCIENCE (Measure Brain Mechanisms)
**You measure:**
- Cortisol (stress hormone via saliva swabs)
- Heart rate variability (HRV via smartwatch)
- Skin conductance (DIY Arduino sensors ~$50)
- Pupil dilation (smartphone eye-tracking)
- Blink rate patterns (laptop webcam)

**Tech researchers can't:**
- They have no neuroscience training
- They don't measure physiological responses
- They don't connect engagement to neurochemistry

**Economists can't:**
- They treat emotion as deviation from rationality
- They don't measure biological mechanisms
- They miss why emotion FORCES irrational choice

### Domain 3: ECONOMICS (Measure Decision-Making)
**You measure:**
- Purchasing impulse (% who buy, amount spent)
- Decision-making quality (rational vs. emotional)
- Decision speed (fast = low critical thinking)
- Decision confidence (low confidence = manipulated)

**Neuroscientists can't:**
- They measure brain activation, not economic behavior
- They don't know if lab findings affect real purchasing
- They lack the framework to measure economic consequences

**Tech researchers can't:**
- They optimize for engagement, not purchase quality
- They don't measure whether purchases are beneficial to user
- They don't understand the economic irrationality

## YOUR RESEARCH STRATEGY: LEVERAGE THE GAP

### Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Prove Effect Across Domains
**Question:** Does algorithmic fractionation affect psychology, brain, and purchasing simultaneously?
**Measure:** All three domains in one RCT
**Find:** Yes—emotional sequence causes blink rate drop + cortisol spike + 60% increased purchasing
**Publication:** First paper establishes the effect

### Phase 2 (Months 6-12): Mechanism Across Domains
**Question:** What's the neurochemical mechanism connecting psychology to purchasing?
**Measure:** Temporal relationship between brain changes → behavioral changes
**Find:** Cortisol elevation → prefrontal disengagement → impulsive purchasing
**Publication:** Explain HOW it works

### Phase 3 (Months 12-18): Individual Differences
**Question:** Who's most vulnerable across all three domains?

### Phase 4 (Months 18-24): Interventions Across Domains

**Publication:** Enable solutions
