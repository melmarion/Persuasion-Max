## PEER-REVIEWED ACADEMIC SOURCES

### Algorithmic Influence & Polarization

1. **Science (2024)** - "Reranking partisan animosity in algorithmic social media feeds alters affective polarization"
   - https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adu5584
   - Key finding: 10-day field experiment with 1,256 participants showed algorithmic content exposure directly alters emotional states by 2+ points on a 100-point feeling thermometer

2. **PNAS Nexus (2024)** - "Engagement, user satisfaction, and the amplification of divisive content on social media"
   - https://academic.oup.com/pnasnexus/article/4/3/pgaf062/8052060
   - Key finding: Engagement-based ranking amplifies emotionally charged, out-group hostile content; users do not prefer algorithmically selected political content

3. **PMC/Psychological Science (2024)** - "Social Drivers and Algorithmic Mechanisms on Digital Media"
   - https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11373151/
   - Key finding: Facebook's algorithm weighted angry emoji reactions 5x higher than likes (2018); anger-evoking material disproportionately includes misinformation

### Persuasion Principles & Social Engineering

4. **Springer (2024)** - "Do Cialdini's Persuasion Principles Still Influence Trust and Risk-Taking When Social Engineering is Knowingly Possible?"
   - https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-59465-6_17
   - Key finding: Study of 642 UK and Arab participants; Social Proof and Authority most influential; effects persist even when influence is knowingly possible

5. **arXiv (2024)** - "The Dark Patterns of Personalized Persuasion in Large Language Models"
   - https://arxiv.org/html/2411.06008v2
   - Key finding: LLMs adapt persuasive language based on personality cues; potential for mass-scale personalized influence

6. **ACIG Journal (2024)** - "Deepfake Influence Tactics through the Lens of Cialdini's Principles"
   - https://www.acigjournal.com/Deepfake-Influence-Tactics-through-the-Lens-of-Cialdini-s-Principles-Case-Studies,201147,0,2.html
   - Key finding: DEEP FRAME tool developed; Authority and Social Proof most commonly leveraged in deepfakes

### Review Detection & Consumer Behavior

7. **Cambridge Core (2024)** - "Recent state-of-the-art of fake review detection: a comprehensive review"
   - https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/knowledge-engineering-review/article/recent-stateoftheart-of-fake-review-detection-a-comprehensive-review/F02E8339C43A62BA63EBD54A1608F785
   - Key finding: BERT-based classification achieves 91% accuracy; Graph Neural Networks effective for reviewer network analysis

### Behavioral Engagement

8. **ScienceDirect (2023)** - "Engineered highs: Reward variability and frequency as potential prerequisites of behavioural addiction"
   - https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0306460323000217
   - Key finding: Variable ratio reinforcement schedules create compulsive engagement; activates dopamine pathways similarly to high-engagement substances

### Interface Design Patterns

9. **ACM CHI (2024)** - "Staying at the Roach Motel: Cross-Country Analysis of Manipulative Subscription and Cancellation Flows"
   - https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3613904.3642881
   - Key finding: Systematic analysis of signup vs cancel asymmetry across platforms

10. **Princeton (2019)** - Dark Patterns Study
    - Key finding: 11% of e-commerce sites employ roach motel patterns; 1.2% reset urgency timers

### Cognitive Load & Decision Fatigue

11. **Journal of Electronic Commerce Research (2025)** - "A Cognitive Load Perspective"
    - http://ojs.jecr.org/jecr/sites/default/files/2025vol26no1_Paper3.pdf
    - Key finding: Working memory limited to 7 +/- 2 units; max processing ~20 seconds; better checkout design increases conversions by 35.26%

12. **Journal of Marketing Research** - Decision Fatigue in E-commerce
    - https://winsomemarketing.com/winsome-marketing/decision-fatigue-in-e-commerce-how-many-product-options-are-too-many
    - Key finding: After 10-15 decisions, analytical capability drops 40%; compliance increases 35% when decision fatigued

### Neuromarketing & Physiological Research

13. **PMC** - "Technological advancements and opportunities in Neuromarketing"
    - https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7505913/
    - Key finding: Blink rate, skin conductance, HRV, and pupil dilation correlate with emotional states and decision-making

14. **PMC** - "Beyond Self-Report: A Review of Physiological and Neuroscientific Methods to Investigate Consumer Behavior"
    - https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6137131/
    - Key finding: Venkatraman et al. (2015) comparison of neuroimaging tools and physiological measures

### Anchoring & Pricing

15. **ResearchGate (2024)** - "Anchoring decisions: the role of decoy pricing in consumer choices"
    - https://www.researchgate.net/publication/383748300_Anchoring_decisions_the_role_of_decoy_pricing_in_consumer_choices
    - Key finding: 70% of consumers influenced by initial price anchor
